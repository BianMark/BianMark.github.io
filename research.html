A EEG-based PD Classification Model

Independent Project
From January 2023 to April 2023

One of the most prevalent neurological brain diseases is Parkinson's disease, which can be diagnosed a long time ago with a variety of clinical methods. In recent years, it has been common practice to use Electroencephalography (EEG) signal analysis to identify dementia in its early stages because of its high speed, low cost, and accessibility. Many novel methods which apply EEG to the diagnosis of Parkinson’s disease are shown to be simple and effective. Recent years have seen the development of EEG signal processing as a key technique for researchers to gather appropriate features for Parkinson's disease diagnosis. In this study, a novel system was created for computer-aided diagnosis that is capable of extracting features from EEG signals and discriminating patients affected by Parkinson’s disease. After perprocessing the EEG data, the Butterworth filter has been used to decompose the signals into four frequency sub-bands. Welch’s PSD features are then extracted as the input of supervised machine learning methods-the k-Nearest Neighbor (KNN) to classify EEG features into Parkinson’s disease (PD) and healthy controls (HC). The 10-fold cross-validation has been employed to validate the performance of this model. The results achieve 98.82% accuracy, 99.19% sensitivity, and 91.77% specificity, respectively. The acquired findings demonstrate the validity of our strategy and that our diagnosis method is improved when compared to earlier research. At last, this novel method may be a supplementary tool for the clinical diagnosis of Parkinson’s disease.

Improvements in the architecture of the AES-128

Research Assistant to Shuyan Zhu, and Yao Liu
From January 2022 to September 2022

After realizing the algorithm in Verilog, we found the multiplication counts with lots of computation time. To improve the speed and frequency of our model, we considered replacing the conventional multiplier with a novel one. In consequence, the School-Book Multiplier (SBM), M-term Karatsuba-like multiplier, Toom-Cook multiplier, and LCBA multiplier were compared. Finally, the 3-way Toom-Cook multiplier was chosen because of its balance of frequency and area.

The conventional S-box employs the look-up table, which will generate 160 copies of the S-box in the 10-order pipeline with 128-bit input. To obtain a compact S-box structure, we use inversion and affine transformation to generate the element in S-box for each input, rather than employing the look-up table. Since the inversion step is performed in GF(2^8), the complexity of calculating a polynomial of 7 degrees mod a polynomial of 8 degrees is fairly high. In consequence, we introduced the Tower Field to the inversion process, which converts the elements of GF(2^8) into GF(((2^2)^2)^2), aiming to represent the elements in GF(2^8) by the basis of GF(2^2). Thus we only need to compute a polynomial of 2 degrees mod a polynomial of 3 degrees, which lowers the complexity significantly.
